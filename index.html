Shin Megami Tensei V Vengeance

Shin Megami Tensei V Vengeance (or just SMTV V for short) is one of those special games that only comes around once every blue moon
It was my first ATLUS game after Persona 5, and I was ready to experience what else their titles had to offer.
It posessed a mood that I had yet to see done in other games. The setting was gloomy and yet full of grand spectacles.
When I first played SMTV on Switch, I could never shake this feeling that nobody here was really my friend. That I was alone in a hostile world much less friendly than that of other RPGs I loved. The protagonist's design was weird and otherworldly, and even if the designs of the demons were familliar to me through Persona 5, it felt strange seeing them wandering around as autonomous entities, far removed from the facets of human cognition I was first introduced to them as.
They did not acknowledge any of the time I had spent growing alongside them in that game, it was like seeing a friend you knew in school who has since forgotten about you.
